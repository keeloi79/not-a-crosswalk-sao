# The Case for Visibility

This document serves as the official archive and context for the **"Not Just a Crosswalk"** project. It catalogs the series of legislative mandates, state-level directives, and judicial rulings between 2025 and 2026 that led to the systemic removal of LGBTQ+ rainbow crosswalks across the United States.

---

## 🏛 The National Context: The "SAFE ROADS" Initiative

In July 2025, U.S. Transportation Secretary Sean Duffy launched the **SAFE ROADS (Safe Arterials for Everyone through Reliable Operations and Distraction-Reducing Strategies)** initiative. This federal guidance claimed that "non-standard" road markings were dangerous distractions, leading several state governors—most notably in Florida and Texas—to mandate their immediate removal.

---

## ☀️ Florida: State Mandates and Deadlines

The Florida Department of Transportation (FDOT) issued a series of directives over the summer of 2025, threatening to withhold millions in state transportation funding if "pavement art" was not removed by early September.

- **Key West (Duval & Petronia Streets):** Despite impassioned protests and calls for "home rule" by Commissioner Sam Kaufman, the state covered the iconic rainbow crosswalk with blacktop on **September 9, 2025**.
  
- **Gainesville (NE First Avenue):** The city's three rainbow crosswalks, added in 2019 to honor the late Pride center director Terry Fleming, were removed by **August 27, 2025**.
  
- **Orlando (Pulse Memorial):** The rainbow crossing marking the site of the 2016 massacre was paved over in the middle of the night by work crews in **August 2025**.
  
- **Miami Beach (Ocean Drive):** Faced a strict removal deadline of **September 4, 2025**, despite resistance from local commissioners who viewed the art as a symbol of safety.

## 🤠 Texas: Executive Orders and Judicial Rulings

On **October 8, 2025**, Governor Greg Abbott directed the Texas Department of Transportation (TxDOT) to ensure all "political ideologies" were removed from Texas streets, specifically targeting symbols like rainbow crosswalks.

- **Austin (Fourth & Colorado Streets):** The city complied with the governor's 30-day removal order for its long-standing rainbow crosswalks and the "Black Artists Matter" mural on Eleventh Street.
  
- **San Antonio (Main & Evergreen):** On **January 9, 2026**, a Bexar County judge denied a restraining order to block the removal of the crosswalk in the Pride Cultural Heritage District. The city began the removal process on **January 12, 2026**, opting to install "rainbow sidewalks" as a compromise that did not violate TxDOT travel lane regulations.
  
- **Houston (Montrose):** Rainbow crosswalks in this historically gay neighborhood were removed in **October 2025**.
  
- **Dallas (Cedar Springs Road):** In **March 2026**, crews scraped up the rainbow crosswalks in the Oak Lawn "gayborhood" after an appeal to TxDOT was rejected.

## 💻 The Engineering Response: Digital Persistence

While physical landmarks can be paved over, the **"Not Just a Crosswalk"** SAO utilizes hardware-level persistence to ensure these symbols remain lit and unerasable.

1. **Non-Volatile Memory:** By utilizing the **CH32V003's internal Flash (address `0x08003FC0`)**, the badge "remembers" its identity through power cycles.
   
2. **Logic-Based Resistance:** The `LOAD_SETTINGS` function ensures that even if the badge is disconnected, the user's chosen colors are restored the moment power returns—acting as a digital landmark that cannot be "paved over" by external authority.
   
3. **Visual Continuity:** The **Startup Rainbow Splash** ensures that every time this hardware "boots," it reaffirms its primary identity before transitioning to the user's saved state.
