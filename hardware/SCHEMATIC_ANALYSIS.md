# Schematic Analysis: Crosswalk SAO v8

The **Crosswalk SAO** is engineered to be more than a decorative peripheral; it is a resilient digital landmark. This analysis covers the power delivery, protection, and logic systems that ensure its colors remain lit in diverse electrical environments.

---

## ⚡ The Power Challenge: 3.3V vs. 5V

Most modern host badges provide a **3.3V rail** via the SAO connector. While the **SK6812D-EC3210R** LEDs can technically operate at 3.3V, doing so results in significant color shift (dimming of the blue and green channels) and decreased overall brightness.

### The Solution: MT3608L Boost Converter

To maintain "Not Just a Crosswalk" visibility, the design utilizes an **MT3608L Boost Converter (U21)**:

*   **Operation:** It takes the **3.3V (Bulk3V3)** from the SAO connector and steps it up to a stable **5V rail** for the LED array.
    
*   **Current Handling:** The converter is supported by a **4.7µH inductor (L1)** and **47µF bulk capacitors (C30)** to handle the rapid current switching required by 18 addressable LEDs.

---

## 🧠 Intelligence: Dual-Power Switching

The badge is designed for dual-use: as an SAO or a standalone USB-C device.

### MOSFET Isolation Logic

To prevent the 5V USB rail from back-feeding into a host badge's 3.3V rail (which could cause permanent damage), an **AO3401A P-Channel MOSFET (Qz)** is implemented:

*   **Automatic Disabling:** When a **5V USB-C** cable is connected, the **SVUSB** signal pulls the gate of **Qz** high via **R16**, effectively cutting off the boost converter's path.
    
*   **Safety First:** This ensures that the **MT3608L** is only active when power is strictly coming from the SAO header.

---

## 🛡️ Field Hardening: ESD & Stability

Conferences are notoriously high-static environments. The "Crosswalk SAO" uses an aggressive protection strategy:

### ESD Protection Strategy

Seventeen **TPD1E05U06DPYR-ES ESD diodes** are placed at every critical entry point:

*   **Buttons:** **LEDFX-PD3** and **LEDPALETTE-PD2** are protected to prevent human static discharge from resetting the MCU.
    
*   **Data Lines:** **SDA, SCL, UART_TX,** and **UART_RX** are all shunted to ground via ESD diodes to preserve signal integrity during high-speed animation updates.

### Decoupling Array

A **20-capacitor array (100nF each)** is distributed across the 5V rail.

*   **Purpose:** This high-density decoupling ensures that the high-frequency "noise" generated by the WS2812/SK6812 PWM controllers does not interfere with the **CH32V003 MCU's** internal timing or Flash writing operations.

---

## 📜 Summary of Design Intent

Every trace on the **Crosswalk SAO v8** is built to defend the message of visibility. The hardware logic supports the firmware's **Flash Persistence**, ensuring that once a color is chosen, it stays—protected by MOSFETs, stabilized by bulk capacitance, and shielded by ESD diodes.
